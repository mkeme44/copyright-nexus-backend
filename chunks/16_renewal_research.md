## Content

### How to Research Copyright Renewal Status

For works published 1930-1963 with copyright notice, you must determine if copyright was renewed during the 28th year after publication.

---

## Step 1: Calculate the Renewal Window

**Formula**: Publication Year + 27 = Start of renewal window

| Published | Renewal Window | If Not Renewed, PD On |
|-----------|----------------|----------------------|
| 1930 | 1957-1958 | Jan 1, 1959 |
| 1940 | 1967-1968 | Jan 1, 1969 |
| 1950 | 1977-1978 | Jan 1, 1979 |
| 1960 | 1987-1988 | Jan 1, 1989 |
| 1963 | 1990-1991 | Jan 1, 1992 |

---

## Step 2: Search Renewal Records

### Primary Resource: Stanford Copyright Renewal Database

**URL**: https://exhibits.stanford.edu/copyrightrenewals

**Coverage**: 
- Books (Class A) published 1923-1963
- Renewal records from 1950-1992

**How to Search**:
1. Search by title (exact and variations)
2. Search by author name
3. Search by registration number if known

**If Found**: Copyright was renewed → Work is IN COPYRIGHT
**If Not Found**: May indicate non-renewal, but verify with other sources

---

### Secondary Resource: Copyright Office Online Catalog

**URL**: https://www.copyright.gov/records/

**Coverage**: 1978-present

**Limitation**: Does not include pre-1978 registrations or renewals directly, but may have supplementary information.

---

### Tertiary Resource: Catalog of Copyright Entries (CCE)

**What It Is**: Published volumes listing copyright registrations and renewals

**Where to Find**:
- HathiTrust Digital Library
- Internet Archive
- Copyright Office (physical)

**How to Use**:
1. Find the CCE volume for the renewal year
2. Look in the renewal section for the appropriate class
3. Search alphabetically by title or author

---

## Step 3: Document Your Search

Record:
- Databases searched
- Search terms used
- Dates of search
- Results (positive or negative)

This documentation supports your copyright determination and choice of rights statement.

---

## Step 4: Interpret Results

**Renewal Record Found**
- Status: In Copyright
- Term: 95 years from publication
- Rights Statement: InC

**No Renewal Record Found After Thorough Search**
- Status: Public Domain (likely)
- Confidence: Medium-High
- Rights Statement: NoC-US
- Note: Document search methodology

**Search Inconclusive**
- Status: Copyright Undetermined
- Rights Statement: UND
- Consider: Requesting Copyright Office search ($)

## Statistics to Consider

- Only ~15% of copyrights were renewed overall
- Only ~7% of books were renewed
- Most works from 1930-1963 are in public domain

If you can't find a renewal after thorough search, non-renewal is statistically likely.
