### Works Published 1930-1963: Copyright Notice Check

**First Question**: Does the work have a copyright notice?

**What is a Copyright Notice?**
A copyright notice typically includes:
- The symbol © OR the word "Copyright" OR the abbreviation "Copr."
- The year of first publication
- The name of the copyright owner

**Examples of Valid Notices**
- © 1945 John Smith
- Copyright 1952 by ABC Publishing
- Copr. 1938 Jane Doe

**Where to Look for Notice**
- Title page or page immediately following
- First or last page of text
- Front or back cover
- Container or label (for recordings)

**If NO Copyright Notice is Found**

For works published 1930-1963 WITHOUT a copyright notice:
- **Status**: Public Domain
- **Reason**: Failure to comply with required formalities
- **Rights Statement**: No Copyright - United States
- **URI**: https://rightsstatements.org/vocab/NoC-US/1.0/
- **Confidence Level**: HIGH (if absence of notice is confirmed)

**Important**: Before concluding no notice exists:
- Check multiple copies if possible
- Notice may have been on dust jacket (now missing)
- Notice may be in unexpected location
- Some works had notice requirements waived

**If Copyright Notice IS Found**

→ Proceed to Renewal Status check (Chunk 05)

Works published 1930-1963 WITH notice required copyright renewal to maintain protection.

**Key Points**
- Notice was MANDATORY for published works in this period
- Omission of notice generally meant immediate loss of copyright
- This was a strict requirement with few exceptions
- The 1976 Copyright Act and later amendments did not retroactively restore copyrights lost due to missing notice
