### When to Use "No Copyright" Rights Statements

Use these statements when your copyright analysis determines the work is NOT protected by copyright in the United States.

---

## NoC-US - No Copyright - United States

**URI**: http://rightsstatements.org/vocab/NoC-US/1.0/

**Use When**:
- Work is in the public domain under U.S. law
- Status in other countries may be unknown or different

**Applies To**:
- Published works before 1930
- Published works 1930-1963 without copyright notice
- Published works 1930-1963 with notice but not renewed
- Published works 1964-1977 without notice
- Federal government works
- Unpublished works by creators who died before 1955
- Unpublished works created before 1905 (if creator unknown)

**User Guidance**: "The Item is in the public domain under the laws of the United States, but its copyright status or terms of use may differ in other countries."

**This is the PRIMARY statement for U.S. cultural heritage institutions.**

---

## NoC-CR - No Copyright - Contractual Restrictions

**URI**: http://rightsstatements.org/vocab/NoC-CR/1.0/

**Use When**:
- Work is in public domain BUT
- Contractual agreements restrict re-use
- Examples: donor agreements, licensing restrictions

---

## NoC-NC - No Copyright - Non-Commercial Use Only

**URI**: http://rightsstatements.org/vocab/NoC-NC/1.0/

**Use When**:
- Work is in public domain BUT
- Your institution only permits non-commercial use
- Note: This is a policy choice, not a legal restriction

---

## NoC-OKLR - No Copyright - Other Known Legal Restrictions

**URI**: http://rightsstatements.org/vocab/NoC-OKLR/1.0/

**Use When**:
- Work is in public domain BUT
- Other legal restrictions exist (not copyright)
- Examples: privacy rights, publicity rights, cultural heritage laws

---

## Alternative: Creative Commons Public Domain Tools

For public domain works, you may also consider:

**Public Domain Mark (PDM)**
- Use for works confirmed to be in public domain worldwide
- Not a waiver - just identification
- URL: https://creativecommons.org/publicdomain/mark/1.0/

**CC0 (Public Domain Dedication)**
- Use when rights holder WAIVES copyright
- Not appropriate for works already in public domain
- URL: https://creativecommons.org/publicdomain/zero/1.0/

---

## Choosing the Right "No Copyright" Statement

| Situation | Statement |
|-----------|-----------|
| Public domain in U.S., standard case | NoC-US |
| Public domain but contractual restrictions | NoC-CR |
| Public domain but institution limits to non-commercial | NoC-NC |
| Public domain but privacy/other legal concerns | NoC-OKLR |
| Public domain worldwide, confirmed | Public Domain Mark |
