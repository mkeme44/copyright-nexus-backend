### How to Determine if a Work Was Published

Under copyright law, "publication" has a specific legal meaning that may differ from common usage.

**Legal Definition of Publication**
Publication is the distribution of copies or phonorecords of a work to the public by:
- Sale or other transfer of ownership
- Rental, lease, or lending
- Offering to distribute copies to a group for further distribution, public performance, or public display

**Key Questions to Ask**

1. **Were copies distributed to the public?**
   - YES → Likely published
   - NO → Likely unpublished

2. **How were copies distributed?**
   - Sold in stores → Published
   - Distributed free to the public → Published
   - Made available for download → Published
   - Given only to specific individuals → May be unpublished
   - Kept in private collection → Unpublished

3. **What was the intent?**
   - Intended for public consumption → Published
   - Created for personal use → Unpublished
   - Limited circulation to specific people → Unpublished

**Common Scenarios**

| Scenario | Status |
|----------|--------|
| Book sold in bookstores | Published |
| Newspaper distributed to subscribers | Published |
| Letter sent to one person | Unpublished |
| Photograph in family album | Unpublished |
| Manuscript submitted to publisher but rejected | Unpublished |
| Thesis deposited in university library | Depends on access policies |
| Speech delivered publicly but never printed | Unpublished |
| Film shown in theaters | Published |
| Home movie shared with family | Unpublished |

**Important Notes**
- Public performance alone does NOT constitute publication
- Public display alone does NOT constitute publication
- The concept of publication was not explicitly defined before 1978
- Courts have been liberal in finding works unpublished when there's doubt

- Determined UNPUBLISHED → Go to Creator Identity analysis
