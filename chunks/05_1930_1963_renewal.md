### Works Published 1930-1963 with Notice: Renewal Check

**Critical Rule**: Copyright renewal was MANDATORY for works published 1930-1963.

**How Renewal Worked**
- Initial copyright term: 28 years from publication
- Renewal had to be filed during the 28th calendar year
- If renewed: Additional 67 years of protection (95 years total)
- If NOT renewed: Copyright expired permanently

**Calculating the Renewal Window**
Example: Work published in 1945
- Initial term: 1945-1972 (28 years)
- Renewal window: Calendar year 1973 (the 28th year)
- If renewed in 1973: Protected until 2040 (1945 + 95)
- If NOT renewed by Dec 31, 1973: Public domain on Jan 1, 1974

**Renewal Statistics**
- Only about 15% of all registered copyrights were renewed
- For books specifically, only about 7% were renewed
- Most works from this period are in the public domain due to non-renewal

**If Copyright WAS Renewed**
- **Status**: In Copyright
- **Term**: 95 years from publication date
- **Expiration**: January 1 of (publication year + 96)
- **Rights Statement**: In Copyright
- **URI**: https://rightsstatements.org/vocab/InC/1.0/
- **Confidence Level**: HIGH

**If Copyright Was NOT Renewed**
- **Status**: Public Domain
- **Reason**: Failure to renew during 28th year
- **Rights Statement**: No Copyright - United States
- **URI**: https://rightsstatements.org/vocab/NoC-US/1.0/
- **Confidence Level**: HIGH (if non-renewal is confirmed)

**If Renewal Status is UNKNOWN**
- **Status**: Copyright Undetermined
- **Action Required**: Research renewal records
- **Rights Statement**: Copyright Undetermined
- **URI**: https://rightsstatements.org/vocab/UND/1.0/
- **Confidence Level**: N/A - Research required
