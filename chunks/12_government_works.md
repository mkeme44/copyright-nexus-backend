### US Government Works

**Key Question**: What level of government created this work?

**FEDERAL Government Works**
- **Status**: Public Domain (in the United States)
- **Reason**: 17 U.S.C. § 105 - No copyright protection for works of the U.S. Government
- **Rights Statement**: No Copyright - United States
- **URI**: https://rightsstatements.org/vocab/NoC-US/1.0/
- **Confidence Level**: HIGH

**What Qualifies as Federal Government Work?**
- Created by federal employees as part of official duties
- Reports, studies, data produced by federal agencies
- Federal court opinions
- Congressional reports
- Federal regulations and laws

**What Does NOT Qualify**
- Works by government contractors (may be copyrighted)
- Works by state or local governments
- Works created by federal employees outside their duties
- Works where government is assignee, not author

**STATE and LOCAL Government Works**
- **Status**: May be copyrighted
- **Reason**: Section 105 only applies to federal works
- **Action**: Evaluate using standard published/unpublished rules
- Apply the appropriate time period analysis

**Important Distinctions**

| Type | Copyright Status |
|------|------------------|
| Federal statute | Public Domain |
| State statute | Varies by state (some claim copyright) |
| Federal court opinion | Public Domain |
| State court opinion | Usually Public Domain but varies |
| Federal agency report | Public Domain |
| State agency report | May be copyrighted |
| City ordinances | May be copyrighted |

**Contractor Works**
Works created by contractors for the government are NOT automatically public domain:
- Check the contract terms
- Copyright may belong to contractor
- Government may have license but not ownership

**International Note**
U.S. government works are only guaranteed public domain in the U.S. Other countries may grant them protection.

**Edicts of Government**
Courts have held that official legal pronouncements (statutes, regulations, judicial opinions) cannot be copyrighted, even at state/local level, as a matter of due process.
