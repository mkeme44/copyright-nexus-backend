### Works Published March 1, 1989 or Later: Modern Copyright

**Key Rule**: Copyright notice is NOT required for works published on or after March 1, 1989.

**Result for All Works in This Period**
- **Status**: In Copyright
- **No formalities required**: No notice, no registration, no renewal needed for protection
- **Rights Statement**: In Copyright
- **URI**: https://rightsstatements.org/vocab/InC/1.0/
- **Confidence Level**: HIGH

**Duration of Copyright**

For works by individual authors:
- **Term**: Life of the author + 70 years
- Copyright expires on January 1 of the year after the 70th anniversary of author's death

For works made for hire, anonymous, or pseudonymous works:
- **Term**: 95 years from publication OR 120 years from creation (whichever is shorter)

**Examples**

| Type | Author Dies/Created | Expires |
|------|---------------------|---------|
| Individual author | Dies 2020 | January 1, 2091 |
| Joint authors | Last author dies 2025 | January 1, 2096 |
| Work for hire published 1995 | N/A | January 1, 2091 |
| Work for hire published 2020 | N/A | January 1, 2116 |

**What This Means for Cultural Heritage**

Most modern works will be in copyright for the foreseeable future:
- A work published in 1990 by a living author won't enter public domain until 70 years after their death
- Works for hire from 1990 won't enter public domain until 2086 at the earliest

**Why Notice Still Matters**

Although not required, notice provides benefits:
- Defeats "innocent infringement" defense
- Identifies copyright owner for permissions
- Identifies year of first publication

**Determining If Work Is "Made for Hire"**

A work is "made for hire" if:
1. Created by an employee within scope of employment, OR
2. Specially ordered/commissioned AND falls within specific categories AND there's a written agreement

Categories for commissioned works made for hire:
- Contributions to collective works
- Parts of motion pictures
- Translations
- Supplementary works
- Compilations
- Instructional texts
- Tests and answer materials
- Atlases
