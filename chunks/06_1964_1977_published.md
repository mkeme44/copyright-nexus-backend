### Works Published 1964-1977: Notice Check (Renewal Automatic)

**Key Change**: Congress amended the law in 1992 to make renewal AUTOMATIC for works published 1964-1977.

**First Question**: Does the work have a copyright notice?

**If NO Copyright Notice**
- **Status**: Public Domain
- **Reason**: Notice was still required in this period
- **Rights Statement**: No Copyright - United States
- **URI**: https://rightsstatements.org/vocab/NoC-US/1.0/
- **Confidence Level**: HIGH (if absence confirmed)

**If Copyright Notice IS Present**
- **Status**: In Copyright
- **Term**: 95 years from publication
- **Renewal**: Automatic (no filing required)
- **Rights Statement**: In Copyright
- **URI**: https://rightsstatements.org/vocab/InC/1.0/
- **Confidence Level**: HIGH

**Calculating Expiration**

| Publication Year | Expires |
|------------------|---------|
| 1964 | January 1, 2060 |
| 1965 | January 1, 2061 |
| 1970 | January 1, 2066 |
| 1975 | January 1, 2071 |
| 1977 | January 1, 2073 |

**Why This Period is Different from 1930-1963**
- Copyright Renewal Act of 1992 made renewal automatic
- No need to research renewal records
- Only need to verify copyright notice was present
- If notice present → In copyright for 95 years

**What Copyright Notice Looks Like**
- © 1970 Publisher Name
- Copyright 1965 by Author Name
- Copr. 1972 Company Inc.

**Where to Find Notice**
- Title page or verso
- First or last page
- Cover or container

**Benefits of This Automatic Renewal**
- Simplifies copyright determination
- No renewal research needed
- Clear 95-year term from publication
