### Works Published Before 1931: PUBLIC DOMAIN

**Rule**: All works published in the United States before January 1, 1931 are in the public domain.

NOTE: This cutoff advances by one year every January 1. As of 2026, the threshold is 1931.
Works published in 1930 entered the public domain on January 1, 2026.

**Why?**
- Maximum copyright term was 95 years (28 years + 67-year renewal)
- 1930 + 95 = 2025, so works from 1930 entered public domain January 1, 2026
- 1929 + 95 = 2024, so works from 1929 entered public domain January 1, 2025
- Everything published before 1931 is now public domain (as of 2026)

**No Additional Research Needed**
- Copyright notice: Does not matter
- Renewal status: Does not matter
- Author's death date: Does not matter

**Result**
- **Status**: Public Domain
- **Rights Statement**: No Copyright - United States
- **URI**: https://rightsstatements.org/vocab/NoC-US/1.0/
- **Confidence Level**: HIGH

**Digital Reproduction Guidance**
- Faithful digital reproductions of public domain works remain in the public domain
- Use Public Domain Mark, NOT Creative Commons licenses
- Your institution does NOT gain new copyright by digitizing

**What Users Can Do**
- Copy, distribute, display, perform without permission
- Create derivative works
- Use commercially
- No attribution legally required (though often appreciated)

**Common Examples**
- Books published before 1931
- Newspapers and magazines from before 1931
- Sheet music published before 1931
- Photographs published before 1931
- Maps and technical drawings from before 1931

**Note on Foreign Works**
This determination applies to U.S. copyright status. Works may have different status in other countries. The URAA restored some foreign copyrights, but not for works this old.
