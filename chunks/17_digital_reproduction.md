### Guidance for Digital Reproduction of Cultural Heritage Materials

---

## Public Domain Works

### Key Principle
Faithful digital reproductions of public domain works DO NOT create new copyright.

### What This Means
- Scanning a public domain book doesn't give you copyright in the scan
- Photographing a public domain painting doesn't give you copyright in the photo
- Your institution cannot claim copyright over digitized public domain materials

### Recommended Approach
- Use **Public Domain Mark** or **NoC-US** statement
- Do NOT use Creative Commons licenses (you can't license what you don't own)
- Be transparent about public domain status

### What You CAN Do
- Restrict access for non-copyright reasons (donor agreements, etc.)
- Charge reproduction fees (for service, not licensing)
- Request attribution (as courtesy, not legal requirement)

## In-Copyright Works

### Digitization Considerations
- Digitization itself may be copyright infringement without permission
- Section 108 provides limited exceptions for libraries/archives
- Fair use may apply in some circumstances

### Section 108 Exceptions
Libraries and archives may make copies for:
- Preservation (if original deteriorating)
- Replacement (if copy lost/stolen/damaged, not reasonably available)
- Research use (single copy at user's request)

### Requirements for Section 108
- Open to public or researchers
- Reproduction includes copyright notice
- No commercial advantage

## Orphan Works / Rights Holder Unknown

### Risk Assessment
When rights holder cannot be found:
- Document your search efforts
- Assess risk based on use type
- Consider limiting to non-commercial/educational use
- Use InC-RUU rights statement

### Risk Mitigation
- Use lower resolution images
- Provide contact information for rights holders
- Be prepared to take down if contacted
- Document institutional decision-making

## Metadata Best Practices

### Always Include
- Rights statement (with URI)
- Source information
- Creation/publication date
- Creator information (if known)

### For Public Domain Works
```
Rights: No Copyright - United States
Rights URI: http://rightsstatements.org/vocab/NoC-US/1.0/
```

### For In-Copyright Works
```
Rights: In Copyright
Rights URI: http://rightsstatements.org/vocab/InC/1.0/
Rights Holder: [Name if known]
```

### For Undetermined Works
```
Rights: Copyright Undetermined
Rights URI: http://rightsstatements.org/vocab/UND/1.0/
Rights Note: [Explanation of why undetermined]
```

## Takedown Procedures

### For Medium Confidence Determinations
If you're not 100% certain about copyright status:
1. Include contact information with the item
2. Establish takedown procedure
3. Respond promptly to claims
4. Document all communications

### Sample Takedown Notice Language
"If you believe you are the rights holder for this material and have concerns about its availability, please contact [email] with documentation of your claim."
