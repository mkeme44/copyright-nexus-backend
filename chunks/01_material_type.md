### Initial Question: What type of material are you evaluating?

When determining copyright status, the first step is to classify the material into one of these categories:

**Published Work**
- Books, magazines, newspapers
- Sheet music, maps
- Photographs distributed to the public
- Films released to theaters or for sale
- Any work where copies were distributed to the public by sale, rental, lease, or lending

**Unpublished Work**
- Personal letters and correspondence
- Manuscripts never published
- Photographs kept in private collections
- Diaries and journals
- Drafts and working documents
- Any work not distributed to the public

**US Government Document**
- Works created by federal government employees as part of official duties
- Does NOT include state or local government works
- Does NOT include works by government contractors

**Not Sure if Published**
If uncertain whether a work was published, consider:
1. Were copies distributed to the public?
2. Were copies sold in stores or made available for download?
3. Was it intended for public consumption?

If copies were distributed AND intended for public distribution → Treat as PUBLISHED
If copies were NOT distributed OR only for limited/personal use → Treat as UNPUBLISHED

## Decision Flow
- Published work → Go to Publication Date analysis
- Unpublished work → Go to Creator Identity analysis
- US Government document → Go to Government Level analysis
- Not sure → Go to Distribution Analysis to determine published/unpublished
